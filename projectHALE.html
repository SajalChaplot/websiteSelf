Improving Employee Wellness Engagement With Digitisation

Infosys' Indian offices' employ more than 1.5 lakhs of employees , a population nearly equal to Shimla. That's right, a whole city working for one company, it is a big feat. However, with such a scale, it became difficult for the company to understand the employees' wellness needs and evolve the wellness program. It had become obsolete!

I became part of a reimagination project of the wellness program aimed at improving wellness culture and productivity.

My Role


UX Research & Design

I collaboarted with 5 designers to plan and conduct stakeholder workshops, user research and develop insights for the future of Wellness Program. Post discovery phase, I along with another designer, majorly worked on the mobile solution and collaborated with developers for building the platform.

Challenge


Improving User Engagement With Digital First Approach

With limited existing digital strategy, it was impossible for the company to analyse employees needs and offer personalized wellness program. Our goal was to improve employees wellness by enabling better engagement with the wellness program, personlized to their needs.


Project Goals

Our High-level Goals were :

1. Understanding the employees across geographies
2. Transforming Needs into a digital strategy
3. Design a mobile solution that seemlessly integrates into the lives of employees
4. Measue the engagement




Obstacles

Every journey has barriers, and our's were -

1. Humongous Scale
Understanding Employees with a wide range of demographics across different geographies were the biggest roadblock in the initial stages.

2. Communicating Design Outcomes
With the usual lack of awareness about design research outcomes and their impact amongst the stakeholders, it was evident that we will have to communicate more often that design is 'not just churning beautiful screens'.

3. Shipping Designs to Building the Solution
As often happens in an IT project, not all design ideas can be built, and we realized, an agile methodology would have sped up the entire journey.
